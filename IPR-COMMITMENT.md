## Purpose

RF-100 is intended to be an **open, implementable standard**. Remnant
Fieldworks Inc. ("Remnant") wants any party to be able to build, use, and
distribute systems that conform to RF-100 without fear of a patent ambush —
while Remnant retains the right to protect its **brands, products, hosted
services, reference code, and certification authority** from misappropriation.

This document describes the intended patent posture for RF-100. It addresses
**patent rights only**. The specification **text** license (CC BY 4.0) and the
trademark position are stated separately in RF-100 Section 0.1 and are not
altered or expanded by this document.

---

## 0. Definitions

For purposes of this commitment:

- **"Remnant"** means Remnant Fieldworks Inc., an Ohio corporation, and its
  **successors and assigns**.
- **"Remnant Patents"** means any patent claims that Remnant **owns or Controls**
  and that would necessarily be infringed by implementing the Normative
  Requirements of a released version of RF-100. It does not include claims
  reading on Reserved Fields (Section 3) except to the extent those claims are
  necessarily infringed by a Conforming Implementation of the Normative
  Requirements.
- **"Control" / "Controlled"** means the right to grant a covenant or license of
  the scope described here without the payment of consideration to, or the
  consent of being unreasonably withheld by, an unrelated third party.
- **"Normative Requirements"** means the individually numbered MUST / MUST NOT /
  SHALL / SHALL NOT / SHOULD / SHOULD NOT / MAY requirements of a specific,
  released version of RF-100, as published by Remnant (see RF-100 Annex D and
  the RF-101 conformance clause).
- **"Conforming Implementation"** means a product or system that meets the
  Normative Requirements of a released version of RF-100 and does not
  misrepresent its conformance.
- **"Implementer"** means any party that makes, has made, uses, sells, offers to
  sell, imports, or distributes a Conforming Implementation, together with that
  party's suppliers, distributors, and customers acting in that capacity.
- **"Reserved Fields"** has the meaning in Section 3.

## 1. Patent Grant (Royalty-Free, Worldwide, Scoped, Reciprocal) — intended

Subject to finalization after counsel review, Remnant intends to covenant that
it will **not assert** any Remnant Patents against any Implementer, anywhere in
the world, for that Implementer's making, having made, use, sale, offer for
sale, importation, or distribution of a **Conforming Implementation** of a
released version of RF-100, provided all of the following hold:

1. **Conformance.** The implementation meets the Normative Requirements of a
   released RF-100 version and does not extend, modify, or misrepresent those
   requirements in a way that fragments interoperability or misattributes the
   standard.

2. **Reciprocity (defensive termination).** The Implementer (and its affiliates)
   does not initiate or voluntarily join a patent assertion against Remnant, its
   customers, or other Implementers relating to RF-100 or to any Conforming
   Implementation. This covenant terminates automatically, as to any party that
   initiates such an assertion, as of the date the assertion is filed.

3. **Good faith.** The implementation is bona fide technical work, not an attempt
   to appropriate Remnant's trade secrets, proprietary code, trademarks, service
   marks, or trade dress.

**No royalties or fees** are required for the covenant in this Section.

---

## 1A. Binding effect, irrevocability, and beneficiaries — intended

1. **Runs with the patents.** This covenant is intended to be a **binding
   encumbrance that runs with the Remnant Patents.** Any assignment, sale,
   exclusive license, or other transfer of a Remnant Patent shall be **made
   subject to this covenant**, and Remnant shall require each transferee to
   assume it in writing for the benefit of Implementers. A transferee takes
   subject to this covenant whether or not it expressly assumes it.

2. **Successors and assigns.** This covenant binds Remnant's successors and
   assigns, including any acquirer of Remnant or of the Remnant Patents, and any
   entity into which Remnant is merged or reorganized.

3. **Irrevocability / version lock.** For each **released version** of RF-100,
   the covenant published with that version is intended to be **irrevocable and
   perpetual as to implementations of that version.** Remnant may revise this
   commitment only **prospectively for future RF-100 versions**; a revision does
   not diminish the covenant already attached to a previously released version.

4. **Third-party beneficiaries.** Implementers are intended **third-party
   beneficiaries** of this covenant and, once finalized, are intended to be
   entitled to rely on and enforce it according to its terms.

## 2. What is NOT covered by this grant

This patent commitment is intended to apply **only** to Conforming
Implementations of the RF-100 Normative Requirements. Remnant reserves all
rights to enforce its intellectual property against, without limitation:

- **Non-conforming or derivative systems** that copy Remnant's proprietary
  ExecutionProof™, VaultProof™, or ProofRecord™ products, code, UI, or services
  without implementing RF-100, or while misrepresenting conformance.
- **Patent-assertion entities** and other parties that assert patents against
  Remnant, its customers, or the RF-100 ecosystem (see Section 1(2)).
- **Trademark infringement, trade-dress violation, or false endorsement**
  (e.g., use of Remnant's brand names or program marks, or creation of
  confusingly similar products).
- **Trade-secret misappropriation or unauthorized copying** of Remnant's hosted
  platform, reference-implementation source code, or internal tooling.

Certification under RF-101 / RF-102 and use of "RF-100 Certified by Remnant
Fieldworks" or similar program marks require **separate written authorization**
and are **not** granted by this commitment.

## 3. Reserved Fields — Trademarks, Software, and Services

The following are **Reserved Fields.** No rights are granted by this document to:

- **Marks:** Remnant Fieldworks™, ExecutionProof™, VaultProof™, ProofRecord™,
  Proof Before Power™, and Verification Before Execution™, and any RF-100
  conformance/program marks — as trademarks or service marks. (The legal
  structure of any conformance-certification program and its marks — including
  whether it is operated through an independent certifying body — is a **pending
  item; see Annex IPR-A**. These are referred to here as "program marks," not as
  statutory "certification marks," pending that determination.)
- **Software & services:** Remnant's hosted services, APIs,
  reference-implementation code, documentation, and trade secrets.
- **Program assets:** Remnant's conformance/certification program, training
  materials, and conformance-testing tools.

These remain the exclusive property of Remnant Fieldworks Inc. and are licensed
separately under commercial or open-source terms as applicable.

## 4. Specification Text License (cross-reference)

The **text** of the RF-100 specification is made available under **CC BY 4.0**
(Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International), as stated in RF-100
Section 0.1. This IPR commitment addresses **patent rights only** and does not
alter or expand the CC BY 4.0 license or grant any trademark rights.

---

## 5. No Implied Rights; No Exhaustion Beyond Express Covenant

No rights are granted by implication, estoppel, or otherwise except the express
covenant in Section 1. The covenant is **not** a license to any Remnant Patent
for non-conforming uses or for any Reserved Field, and confers no rights with
respect to activities outside the making, using, or distributing of a Conforming
Implementation.

---

## 6. No Warranty / Limitation of Liability

This commitment, when finalized, will be made without warranty of any kind.
Remnant does not warrant that implementing RF-100 will avoid infringing
third-party patents outside Remnant's Control, and Implementers assume all risk
and responsibility for their own IP clearance.

---

## 7. Governing Law

This commitment, when finalized, is intended to be governed by the laws of the
**State of Ohio, U.S.A.**, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles, and
the parties intend the state and federal courts located in Ohio to be the forum
for any dispute (subject to any arbitration provision counsel may add in the
binding version).

## 8. Versioning

The effective version of this commitment for any implementation is the version
published at the time the corresponding RF-100 version was released (see the
version-lock rule in Section 1A(3)). This draft (0.2-draft) accompanies the
RF-100 v1.0 Public Review Draft and will be superseded by a counsel-reviewed
version prior to any certification launch.
