## Purpose

RF-100 is intended to be an **open, implementable standard**. Remnant
Fieldworks Inc. ("Remnant") wants any party to be able to build, use, and
distribute systems that conform to RF-100 without fear of a patent ambush —
while Remnant retains the right to protect its **brands, products, hosted
services, reference code, and certification authority** from misappropriation.

This document describes the intended patent posture for RF-100. It addresses
**patent rights only**. The specification **text** license (CC BY 4.0) and the
trademark position are stated separately in RF-100 Section 0.1 and are not
altered or expanded by this document.

---

## 1. Patent Grant (Royalty-Free, Scoped, Reciprocal) — intended

Subject to finalization after counsel review, Remnant intends to commit that it
will **not assert** any patent claims owned or controlled by Remnant against any
party for that party's **implementation, use, or distribution of systems
conforming to the published normative requirements of RF-100**, provided all of
the following hold:

1. **Conformance.** The implementation follows the normative requirements
   published in RF-100 (as versioned and maintained by Remnant Fieldworks) and
   does not extend, modify, or misrepresent those requirements in a way that
   fragments interoperability or misattributes the standard.

2. **Reciprocity (defensive termination).** The implementer does not initiate a
   patent assertion against Remnant Fieldworks, its customers, or other RF-100
   implementers for their RF-100 conformance work. This non-assertion commitment
   terminates automatically as to any party that initiates such an assertion.

3. **Good faith.** The implementation is bona fide technical work, not an
   attempt to appropriate Remnant's trade secrets, proprietary code,
   trademarks, certification marks, or trade dress.

---

## 2. What is NOT covered by this grant

This patent commitment is intended to apply **only** to conforming
implementations of the RF-100 standard. Remnant reserves all rights to enforce
its intellectual property against, without limitation:

- **Non-conforming or derivative systems** that copy Remnant's proprietary
  ExecutionProof™, VaultProof™, or ProofRecord™ products, code, UI, or services
  without implementing RF-100, or while misrepresenting conformance.
- **Patent assertion entities and bad actors** that attack Remnant, its
  customers, or the RF-100 ecosystem.
- **Trademark infringement, trade-dress violations, or false endorsement**
  (e.g., using Remnant's brand names or certification marks, or creating
  confusingly similar products).
- **Trade-secret misappropriation or software piracy** (copying Remnant's
  hosted platform, reference-implementation source code, or internal tooling).

Certification under RF-101 / RF-102 and use of "RF-100 Certified by Remnant
Fieldworks" or similar marks require separate authorization and are **not**
granted by this commitment.
